I am a PhD candidate in Environmental Engineering at Duke University studying structure–property–sustainability relationships for engineered nanomaterials with applications in antimicrobial technologies, environmental remediation, and energy. My work integrates material design and advanced characterization (XPS, XRD, TEM), experimental reactor design, and life cycle assessment (SimaPro) to develop sustainable, high-performing carbon nitride and carbon nanoparticle materials. I lead LCA studies, develop novel material selection frameworks, and collaborate with industry partners on sustainability screening and decarbonization pathways.

Selected research

  • Tuning reactive species production of graphitic carbon nitride — oxygen doping strategies for enhanced visible-light activity (Duke University).
  • Respiratory pathogen inactivation in drinking water — reservoir reactor achieving >99.99% photocatalytic inactivation of P. aeruginosa.
  • Life cycle assessment for scaled-up nanoparticle production — LCA hotspots and decarbonization for carbon nanoparticle and CN material processes (industry collaborations).
