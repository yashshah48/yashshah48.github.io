About

I am a chemical engineer by training and currently a Ph.D. candidate in Environmental Engineering at Duke University with over seven years of experience across environmental catalysis, materials design and characterization, and life cycle assessment (LCA). I have led collaborative LCA projects with academic and industry partners, including BASF Corporation, with findings published in Environmental Science & Technology and Sustainable Chemistry & Engineering. My doctoral research establishes structure–property–sustainability relationships for engineered nanomaterials in antimicrobial, environmental, and energy applications.

I earned dual Bachelor’s and Master’s degrees in Chemical Engineering from the National Institute of Technology (NIT) Rourkela, India (Class of 2021), graduating Rank 1 with the Institute Silver Medal for academic excellence. During my studies, I completed multiple research internships: with Dr. Taraknath Das at the Indian Institute of Technology Roorkee on catalytic oxidation of volatile organic compounds; with Dr. Jafar Soltan at the University of Saskatchewan on designing catalytic ozonation reactors for the removal of recalcitrant water pollutants; and with Dr. Sujit Sen at NIT Rourkela on the green synthesis of metal oxide catalysts derived from plant leaf extracts.
