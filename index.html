Researching water systems for a climate-resilient future

My group studies contaminant transport in urban watersheds, adaptive design for critical infrastructure, and nature-based solutions to improve water quality and resilience.

Current projects include sensor networks for real-time water quality monitoring, coupled hydrologic-hydraulic contaminant models, and lifecycle assessments for low-impact infrastructure. I teach undergraduate and graduate courses on environmental fluid mechanics, water resource systems, and data-driven environmental modeling.

Research

Short overview of themes, goals, and methods.

Resilient Urban Water Systems
Developing hybrid nature-based and engineered solutions for flood and contaminant management. Methods: field sensors, numerical modeling, uncertainty quantification
Real-time Water Quality Monitoring
Low-cost sensor networks and statistical data-fusion to detect pollutant events and inform rapid response.
Transport & Fate of Emerging Contaminants
Laboratory and mesocosm experiments coupled with reactive-transport modeling to understand persistence and remediation strategies.
