Publications — LCA-related papers

LCA-focused publications and short notes on what the life cycle assessment delivered for each study.

Toward Sustainable Scaled-Up Synthesis of Lignin-Derived Carbon Nanoparticles Using a Furnace Aerosol Reactor

S. Modi, Yash Shah, O. Okonkwo, L. M. Gilbertson, and P. Biswas
DOI: 10.1021/acs.est.5c06572
What value LCA added?

LCA quantified cradle-to-gate environmental hotspots for scaled-up lignin-derived carbon nanoparticle production, identifying energy use, precursor sourcing, and material yield as dominant contributors. This evidence led to prioritization of process interventions (e.g., energy source switching and yield improvements) and guided scale-up decisions with quantified impact reductions.

Light-Driven Chemical Cascade Reduces Barriers to Hydrogen Production

V. R. Battula, G. Mark, M. S. Naeem, Yash Shah, M. Volokh, L. M. Gilbertson, N. López, and M. Shalom
DOI: 10.1021/jacs.5c07557
What value LCA added?

The LCA placed laboratory-scale advances for light-driven hydrogen production into a systems context and compared alternative pathways. It clarified trade-offs between material and catalyst intensity and lifecycle energy/emissions, informing choices for pathway selection and priorities during scale-up.

Balancing Functional Properties and Environmental Impact of Graphitic Carbon Nitride: A Case Study on Boron Doping Syntheses

Yash Shah and L. M. Gilbertson
DOI: 10.1039/D3EN00633F
What value LCA added?

LCA enabled direct comparison between multiple synthesis routes for boron-doped graphitic carbon nitride. By quantifying electricity and precursor impacts and coupling environmental burdens to material performance, the study recommended synthesis options that balance desired functionality with lower lifecycle impacts.
